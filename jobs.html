Chapter 3

Resume & Portfolio

Your resume gets you the interview. Your portfolio proves you can do the work.

Resume Must-Haves

  • Quantified achievements: "Reduced app launch time by 40%"
  • iOS-specific keywords: Swift, SwiftUI, UIKit, Core Data, Combine
  • Architecture experience: MVVM, Clean Architecture, Coordinator
  • Links to App Store apps and GitHub projects
  • CI/CD and testing: XCTest, Fastlane, Bitrise, GitHub Actions
  • Performance work: memory profiling, Instruments, optimization
  • Team impact: code reviews, mentoring, RFC proposals

Portfolio Must-Haves

  • GitHub profile with pinned iOS projects and clear READMEs
  • At least one App Store app (even a simple utility)
  • Open-source contributions to popular Swift repos
  • Technical blog posts demonstrating deep iOS knowledge
  • Clean commit history showing professional workflow
  • Screenshots or demo videos of your best work
  • Side project using latest APIs (SwiftUI, Swift Concurrency)

Ideal Resume Structure

Header (name, links, location) → Summary (2-3 lines, iOS-focused) → Experience (reverse chronological, bullet points with metrics) → Projects (App Store apps, open source) → Skills (Swift, frameworks, tools) → Education (degrees, certifications). Keep it to one page for junior/mid, two pages max for senior+.

Chapter 4

Dos & Don'ts

Common patterns that separate successful iOS job seekers from the rest.

✓ Do
  • Tailor your resume for each role — highlight relevant iOS experience
  • Contribute to open-source Swift projects to build credibility
  • Build a portfolio app that showcases your best architectural patterns
  • Practice coding problems in Swift, not just Python or JavaScript
  • Network in iOS communities: iOS Dev Weekly, Swift forums, local meetups
  • Study the company's app before the interview — find bugs, suggest improvements
  • Negotiate every offer — even a 10% bump compounds over your career
  • Keep learning: WWDC sessions, Swift Evolution proposals, new APIs
  • Follow up after interviews with a thoughtful thank-you note
  • Maintain a "brag document" tracking your wins and impact metrics
× Don't
  • Apply to 100 jobs with the same generic resume
  • List every technology you've ever touched — focus on iOS-relevant skills
  • Skip the cover letter when the company explicitly asks for one
  • Badmouth previous employers or teams in interviews
  • Ignore system design prep — it's the #1 filter for senior roles
  • Forget to ask questions — it signals low interest in the role
  • Accept the first offer without negotiating — you're leaving money on the table
  • Neglect soft skills — communication matters as much as code quality
  • Ghost recruiters or interviewers — the industry is smaller than you think
  • Stop learning after getting hired — complacency kills career momentum

Chapter 5

Interview Process

The typical iOS interview pipeline from application to offer. Know what's coming at each stage.

📋

Resume Screen

Recruiter reviews your resume and portfolio. iOS keywords and App Store links matter most.

1 – 3 days
📞

Phone Screen

30-minute call with recruiter or hiring manager. Culture fit, salary expectations, and basic technical questions.

30 min
💻

Technical Round

Live coding or take-home project in Swift. Algorithms, data structures, and iOS-specific problems.

60 – 90 min
🏗️

System Design

Design an iOS app architecture. Networking layer, caching, offline support, modularization.

45 – 60 min
🤝

Culture Fit

Behavioral questions using STAR method. Teamwork, conflict resolution, leadership examples.

30 – 45 min
🎯

Offer

Verbal offer followed by written. Review total compensation: base, equity, bonus, benefits.

1 – 2 weeks

Common iOS Interview Topics

Swift Language SwiftUI UIKit Combine Async/Await MVVM Clean Architecture Core Data Networking Memory Management ARC & Retain Cycles Dependency Injection Testing (XCTest) Performance App Lifecycle Modularization

Salary Guide

Market rates for iOS developers by level and region. Use this data to benchmark and negotiate.

Level US (USD) EU (EUR) Remote-Global (USD)
Junior $70K – $100K €35K – €55K $40K – $70K
Mid-Level $100K – $150K €55K – €80K $60K – $110K
Senior $150K – $200K €75K – €120K $90K – $160K
Staff $190K – $280K €110K – €170K $130K – $220K
Principal $250K – $400K+ €150K – €250K+ $180K – $300K+

Ranges represent base salary only (excluding equity, bonuses, and benefits). Based on 2026 data from Levels.fyi, Glassdoor, and Blind. Actual compensation varies by company, location, and negotiation.

Negotiation Tips

  • Always negotiate — companies expect it. The worst they can say is no, and most will meet you partway.
  • Research your market value using Levels.fyi, Glassdoor, and Blind before naming a number.
  • Negotiate total compensation, not just base salary — equity, sign-on bonus, remote stipend, and PTO all have value.
  • If you have competing offers, mention them professionally. "I'm currently evaluating another offer at $X" is a powerful signal.
